Executive Summary
Dear Board Members,
As assigned by the Board, I assumed responsibility as General Manager of Well Land to launch and stabilize the heavy machinery, vehicle, and vessel rental operations. This direct involvement—together with cross-group coordination required to keep operations moving—has highlighted a clear limitation in our current operating model: the Group lacks consistent standards, system visibility, and enforceable controls across subsidiaries, which reduces profitability and increases operational risk.
To deliver the modern, disciplined, and interconnected operation the Board expects, I propose migrating from the current oversized Dynamics 365 Finance & Operations environment to Dynamics 365 Business Central. Business Central will be deployed as a single connected group environment (multi-company), enabling standardized workflows and management-grade reporting while maintaining subsidiary autonomy.
The one-time implementation cost is capped at USD 55,000 (PiNetworks quotation), with a significant reduction in ongoing annual costs compared to remaining on F&O and scaling user counts.

1) What this proposal changes (Operating Model + Org Structure Alignment)
This program is not only an ERP change. It is a Group operating model upgrade aligned to a lean shared-services structure at Antrac Holding.
Target Group Structure (Lean, ERP-aligned)
The BC environment will be governed and supported through these group-level shared service departments:
Finance & Performance (CFO-led) — consolidated reporting, controls, cash, intercompany, and group performance dashboards
People & Payroll (HR-led) — single employee database, attendance capture, payroll processing, and approval handoff to Finance
Supply Chain Management (SCM) (SCM Manager under CFO for controls) — procurement governance, inventory governance (internal-use + resale), and international shipments/clearance governance
ICT & Business Systems (ICT Manager-led) — BC administration, security/roles, user adoption, ICT custody tracking (devices), and support coordination with PiNetworks
Facilities, Safety & Compliance (CFSO-led) — group-wide facilities governance, safety standards, audit readiness, and compliance reporting
Key principle: subsidiaries remain accountable for sales and execution, while shared services enforce standards, controls, and reporting consistency.

2) Scope — All subsidiaries and internal units interconnected in BC
The BC deployment will model Antrac as a multi-company environment, sharing master data and standards while keeping entity-based control and accountability.
2.1 Direct revenue-generating units (priority for sales and margin reporting)
Well Land — rentals + logistics services; fleet utilization, maintenance and repair cost visibility

1. Executive Summary  
This report outlines:  
1. the current state findings  from initial discovery across Well Land’s back -end controls and operational

2. the workstreams in progress  to develop the  action plan  for making Well Land self -sufficient and profit -

• Revenue generation  (enquiry → quote → dispatch → utilization tracking → invoicing → collections), and  
• Expenditure control  (request → sourcing → approval → PO → payment → receiving → inventory/asset cost 
allocation), and  
• Operational visibility  (where staff and assets are, their status, and how work is executed across multiple 
locations/projects without dependence on repeated calls).  
 
2. Purpose and scope of current work  
The BoD direction is to build a fast, practical action plan that can be executed with existing resources while preparing for 
fleet expansion. My scope of work currently focuses on establishing the minimum operating backbone to answer, at any 
time and without ad -hoc phone follow -ups:  
• What services are being sold, at what price basis, to whom, and how jobs convert into cash  
• What is being spent, why, who approved it, what was received, and how it was recorded  
• Where each staff member is and what they are assigned to  
• Where each vehicle/asset is, whether it is working/idle/down, and what project it is attached to  
• What inventory exists, what is committed, and what is moving  
• Which requests and jobs are open and their current stage  
This baseline is essential before formalizing profitability targets, utilization targets, or staffing plans for the expanded fleet.  
3. Key discoveries to date  
3.1 Expenditure and procurement reality (“as -is”) 
Transaction review indicates that purchasing is frequently executed through an informal “quote -to-payment” workflow. In 
practice:  
• operational requests originate verbally (calls/WhatsApp ). 
• quotations are obtained and approvals are often verbal.  
• payments occur via transfer or cash directly against quotations.  
• items are collected and delivered.  
• documentation is inconsistent (PO issuance, supplier invoices, receiving evidence/GRNs, and transaction 
pack filing).  
Impact:  This materially limits traceability, weakens audit evidence, increases leakage risk, prevents accurate inventory 
integrity, and blocks reliable allocation of spend to specific assets/projects.  
 
3.2 Multi -requestor and distributed operations (complexity multiplier)  
Well Land operational demand is raised by more than one requestor and split across functions:  
• Operations/rentals execution  (Janaka)  
• Repairs oversight and parts demand  (Sampath)  
• Malé support and purchasing execution  (Ziyad/Accounts)  
Assets are distributed across multiple locations (e.g., Thilafushi and remote sites such as Goidhoo, Muthaafushi, 
Bodufin olhu, etc.), and vehicles can shift between islands/projects. Without a single system and single source of truth, 
tracking depends on manual chasing (calls) and individual memory.  
Impact:  Higher likelihood of duplicated purchases, unclear ownership of follow -ups, untracked downtime, 
underbilling/overlooked billable time, and delayed decision -making—especially as fleet size increases.  
 
3.3 Revenue process baseline not yet consolidated (in progress)  
The revenue side (enquiries, availability confirmation, quoting, dispatch, utilization tracking, billing and collections) app ears 
to be functioning through informal coordination rather than a documented, standardized process with:  
• a consistent enquiry/lead log,  
• a standard quotation template and approval rules,  
• a baseline rate card/service catalogue (or defined pricing method),  
• consistent rental utilization evidence (timesheets, meter logs, customer signoffs ), 
• defined invoicing triggers and AR follow -up discipline,  
• return/inspection and damage management protocols.  
Impact:  Without a verified revenue process baseline, profitability cannot be reliably measured, utilization cannot be 
optimized, and revenue leakage (unbilled time / disputes / delayed collections) remains likely.  
 
4. How big the issue is (risk statement)  
This is not a “single process gap”; it is a system absence  issue. The current operating model relies heavily on:  
• verbal requests, ad -hoc follow -ups, and individual knowledge,  
• limited transaction documentation discipline,  
• minimal consolidated reporting on fleet status, utilization, downtime, and receivables.  
With the upcoming addition of 10+ vehicles , the current operating style will predictably face scaling failures:  
• inability to locate assets and confirm status fast,  
• increased downtime due to weak repair/parts coordination,  
• uncontrolled spending  and weak receiving/inventory integrity,  
• underbilling due to missing utilization evidence,  
• delayed cash conversion due to inconsistent invoicing support and weak AR rhythm.

with minimum controls and expanding as baseline information is validated.

(procurement/receiving/inventory)

• Establish a disciplined PR → PO → Receiving → Inventory  chain (currently non -existent in consistent form).

o PR (Purchase Requisition)  
o PO (Purchase Order)
